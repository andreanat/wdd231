MO Mata Ortiz Pottery Guide Project Site Plan

Project Site Plan

Site Name

Mata Ortiz Pottery Guide

  • The name clearly communicates the subject (Mata Ortiz) and the focus (guide/education & visiting).
  • Concise, memorable, and suitable for branding and a logo monogram (MO).

Optional domain idea: mataortiz.art or visitmataortiz.com

Site Purpose

Present a welcoming, modern resource that introduces the ceramic tradition of Mata Ortiz in Chihuahua, Mexico. The site provides: a brief history and cultural context, profiles of artists and styles, a beginner’s visit guide (how to get there, etiquette, safety), and ethical buying tips to support artisans directly.

Audience Scenarios

  • Traveler: “How do I visit Mata Ortiz, and what should I expect when meeting artists in their studios?”
  • Collector: “How can I tell authentic Mata Ortiz pottery from reproductions, and what are fair price ranges?”
  • Student: “Who are the key artists and styles, and where can I find images and short bios for a class project?”

Color Schema

Earthy, high-contrast palette inspired by clay, slip, and desert light. Used consistently across headings, accents, and backgrounds.

Chosen to meet AA contrast for text on Sand & white backgrounds.

Typography

  • Headings: Playfair Display (elegant, heritage vibe)
  • Body & UI: Inter (legible, modern UI)

Heading Sample

Mata Ortiz: Clay, Fire, and Form

Body Sample

The village of Mata Ortiz revived ancient Paquimé ceramic traditions in contemporary forms, producing world-renowned pottery characterized by fine coils, hand burnishing, and intricate slip designs.

Wireframe: Home Page

  • Hero: welcoming photo/illustration + “Plan your visit” button.
  • Sections: short intro, featured artists, styles gallery, visit tips.
  • Global: consistent header/nav/footer reused across pages.

Planned Pages

  1. Home: overview + CTA to plan a visit
  2. Artists & Styles: short bios, images, motifs
  3. Plan Your Visit: how to get there, etiquette, where to buy ethically
